Where your social contributions go

Social insurance funds are separate from the tax budget. Each fund is earmarked for a specific purpose.

🏦
Pension Insurance
18.6%
EE: 9.3% · ER: 9.3% · ceiling: €96,600
Funds current retiree pensions (pay-as-you-go). Also covers rehabilitation. Fed. gov. adds ~€119B/yr subsidy. Administered by Deutsche Rentenversicherung.
🏥
Health Insurance
17.1%
EE: ~8.55% · ER: ~8.55% · ceiling: €66,150
Doctor visits, hospitals, meds, preventive care, sick pay (from week 7), mental health. Spouse & children co-insured for free. Administered by your Krankenkasse (TK, AOK, etc.).
💼
Unemployment Insurance
2.6%
EE: 1.3% · ER: 1.3% · ceiling: €96,600
Pays Arbeitslosengeld I (60–67% of net) for up to 24 months. Funds job training & placement. Short-time work (Kurzarbeit) also covered. Run by Bundesagentur für Arbeit.
🧓
Long-term Care
3.6%
EE: 1.8% · ER: 1.8% · ceiling: €66,150
Home care services, nursing home costs, care allowances. Childless (age ≥23) pay +0.6% surcharge (employee only). Administered by Pflegekasse (linked to your Krankenkasse).
⛑️
Accident Insurance
~1.3%
EE: 0% · ER: 100% · no ceiling
Workplace accidents & occupational diseases. Covers treatment, rehab, and compensation. Rate varies by industry risk class. Run by sector-specific Berufsgenossenschaften.
